# PerX Governance Principles

These governance principles translate our core values into operational standards. They are strict operational requirements that guide platform behavior, not marketing language.

## Trust
* **Trust protection takes priority over short-term growth.** Decisions must prioritize ecosystem health over rapid acquisition or revenue spikes.
* **Trust signals must be explainable.** Users must understand the factors influencing their trust scores and reputation.
* **Platform claims must be accurate.** Features and capabilities must not be overstated. Future or simulated features must be clearly indicated.
* **High-risk actions require stronger controls.** Sensitive actions (e.g., identity changes, large transactions, withdrawals) require step-up verification and audit logging.

## Transparency
* **Users should understand transaction states.** Escrow, deal, and payment statuses must be unambiguous.
* **Rules should be visible.** Platform policies and guidelines must be easily accessible.
* **Fees should be disclosed.** All platform and transaction fees must be clearly stated before commitment.
* **Moderation decisions should be recorded.** Actions taken by administrators and moderators must leave an immutable trail.
* **Trust scores should have understandable factors.** Opaque algorithms for critical reputation metrics are prohibited.

## Accountability
* **Actions must be auditable.** Every critical state change in identity, deals, and trust must be logged.
* **Users are responsible for commitments.** Accepted proposals and deal milestones are binding agreements within the ecosystem.
* **Admin actions must be logged.** System administrators operate with high accountability and transparent audit trails.
* **Disputes require documented resolution.** The path to dispute resolution must follow established, fair, and documented procedures.
* **Platform mistakes must be corrected transparently.** The platform takes ownership of errors and remediates them openly.
